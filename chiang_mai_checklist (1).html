Documents & admin
Start: Now → Phase 3

With a Latvian passport you get 30 days visa-free in Thailand. You'll need to extend or do a border run unless you get the Thai citizenship sorted — which is the goal.

Visa & entry
Check Latvian passport visa-free allowance for Thailand
Latvian passport currently gets 30 days visa-free. Apply for a TR visa (tourist visa) from the Thai Embassy in London for 60 days extendable to 90 — much better than arriving on 30 days.
Phase 2
Apply for Thai Tourist Visa (TR) at Thai Embassy London
Single entry TR visa gives 60 days, extendable by 30 days at a local immigration office in Chiang Mai. Apply 4–6 weeks before departure. You'll need passport photos, proof of onward travel, and proof of funds.
Phase 3
Book onward / return flight for visa application
Thai visa applications require proof of onward travel. Book a cheap refundable or flexible flight out of Thailand — you don't have to use it, but you need to show it when applying.
Phase 3
Make physical and digital copies of all documents
Passport, visa, travel insurance, accommodation booking, emergency contacts. One copy in your bag, one stored digitally in Google Drive or email. Losing your passport in Thailand without copies is a nightmare.
Phase 3
Admin before leaving
Notify your UK bank of travel dates
Barclays, Monzo, whoever — let them know you're going to Thailand for 3 months. Cards get blocked for suspected fraud if you don't. Takes 5 minutes on the app.
Phase 3
Sort a Wise or Revolut card if you don't have one
Essential for Thailand. Wise gives you near-perfect exchange rates with no hidden fees. Withdraw cash from Thai ATMs with minimal charges. Do not rely solely on a UK high street bank card in Thailand.
Phase 2
Cancel or pause any UK-based direct debits not needed
Gym membership, subscriptions you won't use. Anything that auto-charges your UK account while you're away drains your buffer. Audit everything and pause what you can.
Phase 3
The TR tourist visa is significantly better than arriving visa-free. 60 days extendable to 90 gives you your full 3 months without a border run. Apply 4–6 weeks before you fly — don't leave it to the last minute.
Health & vaccinations
Start: Phase 2 at mom's

Getting sick in week one of Chiang Mai kills your momentum and your budget. Sort the basics through your GP — most travel vaccinations are free on the NHS if you book a travel health appointment.

NHS travel health appointment
Book a travel health appointment with your GP
Do this in the first week at your mom's. Some vaccines need multiple doses over several weeks — you need enough lead time before July. Tell them you're going to Thailand for 3 months.
Phase 2
Hepatitis A vaccination
Recommended for Thailand. Spread through contaminated food and water — street food is a daily part of life in Chiang Mai. One dose protects you for a year, booster for 20+ years.
Phase 2
Typhoid vaccination
Recommended for Thailand, especially if eating street food regularly. Oral or injection — ask your GP which is current stock. Protects for 3 years.
Phase 2
Tetanus — check your status
You're riding a motorcycle. Tetanus is worth being up to date on. Your GP will check your records — if you had it as a child you're likely still covered but worth confirming.
Phase 2
Hepatitis B — discuss with GP
Your dad is Thai and you're going long term — worth having. Requires 3 doses over 6 months ideally, but accelerated schedules exist. Ask your GP about options given your timeline.
Phase 2
Other health prep
Get travel insurance — non-negotiable
Medical costs in Thailand without insurance can be catastrophic. World Nomads and SafetyWing are the standard options for digital nomads. SafetyWing is cheaper and covers longer stays. Sort this in Phase 3 before you fly.
Phase 3
Pack a basic medical kit
Ibuprofen, antihistamines, rehydration sachets, antiseptic cream, plasters, insect repellent with DEET. Pharmacies in Chiang Mai are excellent but having basics saves unnecessary trips in the first week.
Phase 3
Dental check before you go
Dental treatment in Thailand is actually excellent and cheap — but a dental emergency in week one is a distraction you don't need. Get a checkup at your mom's if it's been a while.
Phase 2
Travel insurance isn't optional. You're riding a motorcycle in a foreign country for 3 months. One hospital visit without cover could wipe out your entire savings buffer. SafetyWing costs roughly £35/month — factor it into your Chiang Mai budget.
Money & banking
Start: Phase 2

Thailand is cheap but you need your money accessible without losing a chunk of it to fees every time you withdraw. Sort your banking setup before you leave — not at the airport.

Open a Wise account and order the card
Best exchange rates available. Free to open, card takes 1–2 weeks to arrive. Use this as your primary spending card in Thailand. Load it from your UK account and spend in Thai Baht with near-zero fees.
Phase 2
Set up a Revolut account as backup
Never rely on a single card abroad. Revolut as a backup means if your Wise card is blocked, swallowed by an ATM, or lost, you're not stranded. Takes 10 minutes to set up.
Phase 2
Notify your UK bank of Thailand travel
Even if you primarily use Wise, keep your UK account accessible. Banks block international transactions as fraud without notice. Takes 5 minutes on the app or a phone call.
Phase 3
Arrive with £200–300 worth of Thai Baht cash
First few days you'll need cash before you find a good ATM setup. Exchange at the airport is terrible — use a Wise or Revolut ATM withdrawal, or exchange at a SuperRich money exchange in Bangkok which has excellent rates.
Phase 3
Set up a way to receive client payments internationally
Wise Business account lets you receive USD, GBP and EUR payments with no fees. Payoneer is the Upwork standard. Set both up before you land your first client — don't scramble to sort this when someone wants to pay you.
Phase 2
Thai ATM fee awareness
Every Thai ATM charges a 220 Baht (~£5) foreign card fee per withdrawal regardless of amount. Withdraw larger amounts less frequently to minimise this. Factor it into your monthly budget.
On arrival
Sort Wise in Phase 2 — not the week before you fly. The card takes time to arrive and you want to test it before you're abroad. Losing access to your money in week one because you didn't set this up is an avoidable disaster.

Thai citizenship process
Start: Phase 2 — complete in Thailand

You have a legitimate claim to Thai citizenship through your father. Your Thai surname on your UK birth certificate is a significant advantage. This won't be fully resolved before July but the process starts now.

Your situation
Born in UK to a Thai father · Thai surname on birth certificate · Parents married after birth · Father lives in Thailand with valid Thai ID and passport · You are 26 and have never renounced Thai nationality · You are legally a Thai citizen from birth.
Step by step
Contact Thai Embassy London — book an appointment
Email consular@thaiembassy.org.uk or visit thaiembassy.org/london. Explain you were born in the UK to a Thai father, have his surname on your birth certificate, and want to register for a Thai birth certificate. Do this in the first week at your mom's.
Phase 2
Gather documents for Thai birth certificate application
Your UK full birth certificate · Your Latvian passport · Your father's Thai national ID card (copy) · Your father's Thai passport (copy) · Your father's house registration (tabien baan) copy · Your parents' marriage certificate. Ask your dad to send copies of his documents.
Phase 2
Ask your dad to get his documents ready in Thailand
He needs to send you certified copies of his Thai ID, passport, and house registration. The fact you have his surname significantly reduces the chance they'll request a DNA test — but be prepared for that possibility.
Phase 2
Attend Thai Embassy appointment — apply for Thai birth certificate
Bring all documents. Be clear and confident about your situation. The Thai surname on your UK birth certificate is your strongest evidence. This is the step that formally establishes your Thai citizenship on paper.
Phase 2
On arrival in Thailand — get added to dad's house registration
Your dad needs to take you to the local district office (Amphoe) to add you to his tabien baan (house registration book). This is required before you can get a Thai ID card. Your dad's cooperation makes this straightforward.
In Thailand
Apply for Thai national ID card at district office
Once on the house registration, go to the district office with your Thai birth certificate and house registration. Your first Thai ID card must be issued in Thailand — cannot be done at the embassy. This is what unlocks everything else.
In Thailand
Apply for Thai passport
Once you have a Thai ID card, you can apply for a Thai passport at the Department of Consular Affairs. Processing takes 2–3 weeks. With a Thai passport you can stay in Thailand indefinitely with no visa runs.
In Thailand
This process changes your entire long term relationship with Thailand. Don't let it distract you from the main plan — start it in Phase 2 with one afternoon at the embassy, then finish it in Thailand with your dad's help. The plan doesn't depend on it succeeding but your long term future is significantly better if it does.
On arrival — first week
July 2026 — Chiang Mai

The first week sets the tone. Don't spend it exploring and settling in at the expense of your routine. You can settle in and work at the same time — you've been doing that at your mom's for months.

Practical setup
Get AIS SIM at the airport — do it before leaving arrivals
There are AIS counters in arrivals at both Bangkok airports. 30-day unlimited data for around 400–500 Baht. Don't leave the airport without a working Thai SIM — you'll need it for the bus, accommodation check-in, and maps immediately.
Day 1
Bangkok to Chiang Mai — overnight train or bus
Overnight train (Hua Lamphong or Bang Sue station) is the best option — sleeper berth, arrives morning, saves a night's accommodation. Alternatively Nakhonchai Air or Green Bus are reliable overnight coaches. Book in advance.
Day 1
Check into your apartment — confirm WiFi speed immediately
Test the upload speed the moment you arrive. Run a speed test. If it's under 20 Mbps upload, have a conversation with the landlord before you unpack. This is non-negotiable for your work.
Day 1
Rent motorcycle — bring international driving permit
Most rental shops around the old city and Nimmanhaemin area. Daily rate around 150–200 Baht for a Honda Click or PCX. Monthly rate significantly cheaper — negotiate. Inspect the bike thoroughly before signing — photograph all existing damage.
Day 2
Find your local market and food spots
Warorot Market, Muang Mai Market, and the Saturday and Sunday Walking Streets are your cheap food anchors. Eating street food consistently keeps your food budget at £8–10/day comfortably. Know where to go before you're hungry and lazy.
Week 1
Find your gym
Chiang Mai has excellent gyms. Muay Thai gyms often double as general fitness gyms and are very cheap — 200–300 Baht/day or negotiate a monthly rate. Fairtex and Santai are well known. The routine doesn't pause because you're in a new city.
Week 1
Re-establish your routine — day one
The routine from your mom's travels with you. No phone first 2 hours, water, study an ad, create, learn. The temptation to explore and settle in is real — resist it. One week of laziness at the start sets a bad tone for three months.
Day 1
Visa extension
Extend TR visa at Chiang Mai Immigration before day 60
Chiang Mai Immigration Office on Airport Road. Extend your 60-day TR visa by 30 days for 1,900 Baht — gives you 90 days total. Go at least a week before expiry. Bring passport, TM6 card, and a passport photo.
Before day 60
Chiang Mai is an easy city to get comfortable in. That's both its greatest strength and your biggest risk. Comfortable and productive are not the same thing. The routine is what keeps you on track — protect it from the first day.
